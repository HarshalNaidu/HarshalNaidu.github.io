Why product owners need to care about quality automation

2016-06-07
  • Management

Automation is an afterthought in so many projects. One of the retrospective that Netflix had after billing migration to cloud was that they underestimated the end to end testing automation flows.

http://techblog.netflix.com/2016/06/netflix-billing-migration-to-aws.html

The best way to tackle this?

Automation as a part of acceptance criteria in sprints.

And this HAS to come from the business owner / product owner. If you let this be at the discretion of the engineering manager, the chances are that the feature will be delivered but it may not be testable using automation.
But wait, there’s more!

For cross system efficiency, you’ll need to have the acceptance criteria for the automation tests can be integrated, driven and run without much dependency. Imagine a scenario where your system is part of the large scale integration. The calling systems should be able to initiate (set prerequisites, data etc.) run automation as a part of their tests. This is when you get end to end optimization.
And if you (as a business owner/product manager) think that you don’t need to drive this? Think again! Cause’ you should not only care about stuff being done right but also demand flexibility and resilience in the engineering and project processes. Overtime, this needs to become your product and program management and design best practice.

And this is only possible if you design the automation right. And for that your developers need to think of automation as a part of their program. They will only think that way when the business demands flexibility!

Yes, there’s going to be some investment but you are going to reap the rewards by fundamentally changing the way you and your engineering counterparts think about quality and flexibility.
And yes, it is going to be hard as a small startup that is facing all sorts of pressures. If you can start early the payoff will be great. But once your systems attain significant complexity and scale the decision to invest becomes that much harder and you pay through your nose for the snake oil that is UI automation or worse yet outsourcing of manual testing just to be able to keep up with the need to scale.

So where do you start?

Well, how about designing the system as an API based platform? Because of the very nature of modern rest APIs you will build in testability, flexibility and resilience will be a part of your design.
